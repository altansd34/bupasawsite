Asake Reacts As He Joins Wizkid, Burna Boy &amp; Davido With O2 Arena Sold-out Show, Video Leaves Many G

Publish date: 2024-08-24
  • Nigeria's rave of the moment Asake has reacted to his recent concert at the prestigious 02 Arena in the UK
  • Asake, who was joined by the likes of Olamide, Tiwa Savage, and Lil Kesh, among others, said it was his first time performing at an arena
  • He further stressed that his latest feat has made him believe dreams do come true, which stirred comments from fans
  • In a conversation with Legit.ng Solomun Lang, an Afrobeats stan, said Asake has achieved a lot, but it would be wrong to rate him alongside Burna Boy, Davido and Wizkid

The YBNL music star Asake, who continues to trend after shutting down the 20k capacity O2 Arena in London, UK, over the weekend, has broken his silence.

Asake, who Olamide signed to his record label, YBNL, in 2022, has gone on to join the likes of Davido, Burna Boy and Wizkid on the list of Nigerian music stars who have sold-out the prestigious arena.

Reacting to his latest achievement, the Lonely At The Top crooner revealed that performing at the O2 Arena was the first time he would believe that dreams do come true.

The singer said he had never been to the 02 Arena or got the chance to watch an artiste perform there.

Asake selling out the O2 Arena in his second breakthrough year is a big achievement for Afrobeats

Solomun Lang, who applauded Asake for selling out the prestigious O2 Arena, said it was not right to rate him alongside the likes of Davido, Burna Boy and Wizkid, who paced the way and had to endure a lot in the industry.

He, however, stressed that Asake has achieved what has never been seen before in the history of Afrobeats.

"Lil Kesh had a similar run and was the first young artist with the hopes of international stardom, but sadly that never went past Nigeria. Naira Marley came and also broke through to the UK with the help of him being in the UK music scene before switching to Afrobeats. But with Asake, it’s like he was destined for this moment. To see an artist sell out the o2 in their second breakthrough year is an extremely big achievement for Afrobeats, and it is even bigger when you put the perspective of his lyrics being mostly Yoruba. I am even pretty sure he is the first Afrobeats artist to fully sell out the O2( using the full 20k capacity), although that may need to be verified against Davido’s 2019 O2 concert. Asake’s rise to fame is something of Nigerian folklore, something many would’ve never seen coming," Solomun Lang said.
