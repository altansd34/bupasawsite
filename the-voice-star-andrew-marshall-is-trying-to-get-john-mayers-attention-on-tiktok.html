The Voice Season 20 artist Andrew Marshall is currently trying to get John Mayer’s attention on TikTok by singing through his entire catalog. The cancer survivor previously met Mayer as part of the Make-A-Wish program, and he also performed one of his songs on the show.

Andrew Marshall Competed on The Voice Season 20

Marshall auditioned for Season 20 of The Voice with a performance of John Mayer’s “Gravity.” Coach Nick Jonas was the only one who turned his chair, so Marshall automatically joined his team. In the Battles, he was stolen by Blake Shelton, before Jonas stole him back in the Knockouts.

The singer, who was 21 at the time of his audition, made it to the Live Playoffs as part of the Top 17. He performed “Put Your Records On” by Corinne Bailey Rae. Unfortunately, Marshall was eliminated at this point and didn’t get to move on in the competition.

As he shared on the show, Marshall was diagnosed with leukemia at age 16. He received treatment for three years and was in remission by the time he auditioned for The Voice. Thanks to Make-A-Wish, Marshall was able to meet John Mayer.

What Is Marshall Up to These Days?

Since appearing on The Voice in 2021, Marshall released the single “Don’t Wish Your Life Away” and debuted an emotional lyric video for the song which featured home videos from throughout the singer’s life.

Recently, Marshall started a project on TikTok to get John Mayer’s attention. He’s been singing through the star’s entire music catalog one song at a time. The project has nine parts so far, with Marshall singing “Love Song for No One” in the most recent video. He’s also covered “Your Body Is a Wonderland,” “Heartbreak Warfare” and more.

Marshall currently lives in Nashville and frequently posts videos detailing his life as a musician in the city on his social media accounts.
