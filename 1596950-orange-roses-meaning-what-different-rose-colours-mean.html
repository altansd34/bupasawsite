Orange roses

The orange rose is a new member of the rose family. Initially, roses were available in red, pink, and white hues. Most orange rose species available today are cultivars or hybrids of rose plants that are genetically modified to become orange.

Orange roses were first introduced in the early 20th century through hybridisation, which involved crossbreeding red and yellow roses, which gave birth to the orange rose. The orange roses have become immensely popular ever since, and you can find a range of orange roses in vivid, deep hues to pale shades of peach and coral.

Meaning of orange roses

Orange roses blend two classic rose colours: red and yellow. Hence, orange rose meanings and symbolisms relate to these two hues. The meanings of orange roses differ from culture to culture but are often associated with passion, energy, enthusiasm, and gratitude. Below are other meanings associated with the orange roses.

  • Desire
  • Fascination
  • Pride
  • Encouragement
  • Warmth
  • Joy

The crossbreeding of red and yellow roses has produced numerous beautiful orange roses. Their tones go from pale hues like pastel peach to intense tones like vermillion orange. Below are some orange rose varieties, each with their symbolism.

What do the different colours of roses mean?

Here is an overview of the meanings associated with different colours of roses:

  • Red: Symbolise romance and passion.
  • Pink: Symbolise admiration, happiness, and love.
  • White: Signify purity, youthfulness, and innocence.
  • Yellow: Represent friendship and Joy.
  • Orange: Represent fascination, enthusiasm, desire, and energy.
  • Lavender: Represent enchantment, splendour, wonder, mystery, and love at first sight.
  • Peach: Symbolise sincerity and gratitude.
  • Blue: Symbolise mystery, aspiration, and admiration.
  • Green: Signify good fortune, new beginnings, rebirth, and relationship growth.
  • Black roses: Signify mourning, mystery, loss, and elegance.
